🧠 What Is Systems Thinking?

In a lab, nothing exists in isolation. The chemistry analyzer is connected to the water system, the reagents, the training of the operator, the temperature of the room, and even the cleaning products used nearby.

Systems thinking means zooming out. Instead of asking "Which component failed?" you ask "What changed in the whole system that made this component fail?"

Your Mission: You'll investigate 5 lab scenarios. Each has an obvious suspect — and a hidden systemic cause. Click nodes to gather clues. Then make your diagnosis. The more clues you collect before diagnosing, the more points you earn.
